Kenya is predominantly a semi-arid country, with about 80% of its land classified as arid or semi-arid. These regions host 25% of the population

Water Demand in Nairobi

Nairobi faces water demand challenges including a rising population, inadequate supply infrastructure, unpredictable rainfall patterns and inefficient water management. To address these issues, machine learning can be employed for forecasting water usage and availability. By analyzing data on weather and various socio-economic patterns, more accurate predictions can be made to optimize resource allocation.

Using The Application

This application offers a user-friendly interface for predicting water usage with ANN and RNN deep learning models. Users input relevant data into a prediction form, which then generates GIS-mapped results over county wards alongside detailed tables summarizing forecasts for the 4 residential zones.

This allows policymakers to visualize future needs spatially and make informed decisions based on precise data analysis. The intuitive design ensures ease of use for both technical experts and non-experts alike, facilitating better resource management across Nairobi's urban landscape.
